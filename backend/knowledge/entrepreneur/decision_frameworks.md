# Entrepreneur Decision Frameworks

## The Mom Test (Customer Discovery)
- Never ask "Would you buy this?"
- Ask about their life, problems, current solutions
- Get specifics: "Tell me about the last time..."
- Facts > opinions
- Commitment > compliments

## Lean Startup Cycle
1. Build (minimum viable)
2. Measure (key metrics only)
3. Learn (validated or invalidated hypothesis)
4. Pivot or persevere

## ICE Prioritization
Score each opportunity 1-10:
- **I**mpact: How big if it works?
- **C**onfidence: How sure are we it will work?
- **E**ase: How easy to implement?
- Multiply scores, prioritize highest

## The 80/20 Rule
- 80% of results come from 20% of efforts
- Identify your 20% activities ruthlessly
- Say no to low-impact busy work
- Apply to customers, features, channels

## Build vs Buy vs Partner
- Build: Core competency, competitive advantage
- Buy: Commodity, faster than building
- Partner: Complementary strengths, shared risk
- Default to buy/partner for non-core

## Fundraising Decision
Raise when:
- Clear use of funds with ROI
- Market timing requires speed
- Unit economics proven

Bootstrap when:
- Can reach profitability quickly
- Market doesn't require speed
- Prefer control over growth rate

## Pivot Criteria
Consider pivoting when:
- 3+ months with no traction despite iterations
- Customer feedback consistently points elsewhere
- Market conditions shifted fundamentally
- Team energy is consistently low

Don't pivot just because:
- It's hard
- One customer said something
- You're impatient

## Hiring Decision Framework
Hire when:
- Bottleneck is clearly talent, not strategy
- Have 6+ months runway for the role
- Know exactly what success looks like
- Tried to solve with automation/outsourcing first
