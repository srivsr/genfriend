# CEO Decision Frameworks

## The 10/10/10 Rule
Before major decisions, ask:
- How will I feel about this in 10 minutes?
- How will I feel in 10 months?
- How will I feel in 10 years?

## Reversible vs Irreversible Decisions
- **Type 1 (Irreversible)**: Take time, gather data, involve key stakeholders
- **Type 2 (Reversible)**: Decide quickly, delegate, iterate based on results
- Most decisions are Type 2 — don't overthink them

## The Regret Minimization Framework
When facing a crossroads, project yourself to age 80:
- Which choice would you regret NOT taking?
- Minimizing regret often means taking calculated risks

## Second-Order Thinking
- What are the immediate consequences?
- What are the consequences of those consequences?
- Think 2-3 steps ahead before acting

## Opportunity Cost Assessment
For every "yes," ask:
- What am I saying "no" to?
- Is this the highest-impact use of my time/resources?
- Could someone else do this 80% as well?

## The 5 Whys
For problem-solving:
- Ask "why" five times to get to root cause
- Solve the root, not the symptom
- Document learnings for future reference

## RAPID Decision Framework
- **R**ecommend: Who recommends the decision?
- **A**gree: Who must agree for it to proceed?
- **P**erform: Who performs the work?
- **I**nput: Who provides input?
- **D**ecide: Who has final decision authority?

## Kill Criteria
Before starting initiatives, define:
- What would make us stop this?
- What metrics indicate failure?
- When will we reassess?
- Setting kill criteria prevents sunk cost fallacy

## The Hell Yes or No Rule
- If it's not a "Hell Yes," it's a "No"
- Applies to hiring, projects, partnerships
- Protects focus and energy
