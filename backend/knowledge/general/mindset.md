# Mindset & Mental Models

## Growth vs Fixed Mindset
**Fixed Mindset**:
- Intelligence/talent is static
- Avoids challenges
- Gives up easily
- Ignores feedback
- Feels threatened by others' success

**Growth Mindset**:
- Abilities can be developed
- Embraces challenges
- Persists through obstacles
- Learns from criticism
- Inspired by others' success

## Cognitive Biases to Overcome
- **Confirmation bias**: Seek disconfirming evidence
- **Sunk cost fallacy**: Past investment shouldn't dictate future decisions
- **Availability bias**: What's memorable isn't always what's probable
- **Dunning-Kruger**: Beginners overestimate, experts underestimate
- **Status quo bias**: Change isn't inherently bad

## Mental Models for Success
- **Compound interest**: Small improvements accumulate
- **First principles**: Break down to fundamentals, rebuild
- **Inversion**: Think about what would cause failure
- **Circle of competence**: Know what you know and don't
- **Optionality**: Preserve choices when uncertain

## Resilience Building
- Reframe failures as learning opportunities
- Build a "failure resume"
- Develop emotional regulation skills
- Create support systems
- Practice self-compassion

## Motivation Understanding
- **Intrinsic**: Doing for its own sake (mastery, autonomy, purpose)
- **Extrinsic**: External rewards (money, status, recognition)
- Intrinsic more sustainable long-term
- Design environment to support desired behaviors

## Fear & Resistance
- Fear is normal signal, not stop sign
- Name the specific fear
- Ask: "What would I do if I weren't afraid?"
- Progressive exposure reduces fear
- Action often precedes motivation

## Identity-Based Change
- "I want to run" vs "I am a runner"
- Vote for the person you want to become
- Small wins build identity
- Environment reinforces identity
- Surround yourself with people who embody target identity
