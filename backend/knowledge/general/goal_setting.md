# Goal Setting Knowledge

## Why Goals Matter
- Provide direction and focus
- Enable measurement of progress
- Increase motivation and commitment
- Filter decisions and opportunities
- Create accountability

## OKR Framework (Objectives & Key Results)
**Objective**: Qualitative, inspirational, time-bound
- Example: "Become a recognized expert in my field"

**Key Results**: Quantitative, measurable outcomes
- KR1: Publish 4 articles in industry publications
- KR2: Speak at 2 major conferences
- KR3: Grow LinkedIn following to 10,000

## Goal Hierarchy
1. **Vision** (10+ years): Who do I want to become?
2. **Long-term goals** (3-5 years): Major milestones
3. **Annual goals**: This year's focus
4. **Quarterly goals**: 90-day sprints
5. **Monthly/Weekly**: Tactical execution

## SMART Goals
- **S**pecific: What exactly will you accomplish?
- **M**easurable: How will you know you succeeded?
- **A**chievable: Is it realistically possible?
- **R**elevant: Does it align with bigger vision?
- **T**ime-bound: By when?

## Goal Setting Process
1. Reflect on values and vision
2. Identify 3-5 key areas of life/career
3. Set 1-2 major goals per area
4. Break into quarterly milestones
5. Define weekly actions
6. Review and adjust regularly

## Common Mistakes
- Too many goals (diluted focus)
- Vague goals (can't measure progress)
- Only outcome goals (no process goals)
- Set and forget (no review cycle)
- Unrealistic timelines (leads to discouragement)

## Maintaining Motivation
- Connect goals to deeper "why"
- Visualize successful completion
- Track progress visibly
- Celebrate milestones
- Build accountability (partner, coach, community)
- Adjust without abandoning

## When to Pivot Goals
- Circumstances fundamentally changed
- Goal no longer aligns with values
- Consistently making no progress despite effort
- Better opportunity emerged
- NOT just because it's hard
