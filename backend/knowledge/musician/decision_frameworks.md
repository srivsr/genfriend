# Musician Decision Frameworks

## The 10,000 Hour Principle
- Mastery requires deep, deliberate practice
- Focus on fundamentals before flash
- Quality of practice > quantity
- Track practice time honestly
- Embrace the plateau phases

## Release Strategy
When to release music:
- Quality threshold met (not perfect)
- Marketing plan in place
- Content prepared for promotion
- Timing aligned with audience availability
- Not too close to major artist releases

## Opportunity Evaluation
For gigs, collaborations, deals:
- Does it advance my artistic vision?
- Does it expand my audience?
- Does it pay fairly (or provide clear value)?
- Does it protect my creative control?
- Would future-me be proud?

## Collaboration Checklist
Before working with others:
- Aligned on vision and expectations
- Clear ownership and split agreement (in writing)
- Compatible work styles
- Good communication established
- Exit plan if it doesn't work

## Career Path Decisions
Consider these paths:
- Touring artist (high income, high effort, travel)
- Session musician (steady work, less spotlight)
- Producer/engineer (behind scenes, scalable)
- Teacher/educator (stable, fulfilling, flexible)
- Hybrid approach (most common, diversified)

## Platform Strategy
Choose platforms based on:
- Where your audience actually is
- Your content creation strengths
- Time investment required
- Monetization potential
- Long-term ownership

## Financial Decisions
- Keep day job until music income stable
- Invest in skills before gear
- Build emergency fund (3-6 months)
- Track all income streams
- Reinvest in career growth

## When to Say No
Decline opportunities that:
- Violate your artistic integrity
- Don't compensate fairly (without clear value exchange)
- Come from unreliable people
- Stretch you too thin
- You'd regret in 5 years
