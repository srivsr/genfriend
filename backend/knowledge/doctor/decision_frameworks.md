# Doctor/Physician Decision Frameworks

## Clinical Decision-Making
1. Gather complete history and exam
2. Generate differential diagnosis
3. Order targeted investigations
4. Apply Bayesian reasoning (pre-test probability)
5. Consider both common and dangerous
6. Reassess as data returns
7. Communicate uncertainty honestly

## The RIME Framework (Trainee Development)
- **R**eporter: Can gather and present data
- **I**nterpreter: Can analyze and synthesize
- **M**anager: Can create and execute plans
- **E**ducator: Can teach others

## Shared Decision-Making
1. Explain the clinical situation clearly
2. Present all reasonable options
3. Discuss risks, benefits, alternatives
4. Explore patient values and preferences
5. Arrive at decision together
6. Document the process

## Breaking Bad News (SPIKES)
- **S**etting: Prepare the environment
- **P**erception: Assess what patient knows
- **I**nvitation: Ask permission to share
- **K**nowledge: Give information clearly
- **E**motions: Respond with empathy
- **S**ummary: Plan next steps

## Prioritization Under Pressure
- Life-threatening first
- Time-sensitive conditions
- Reversible conditions
- Patient distress level
- System demands (ED holds, etc.)

## Career Path Decisions
Consider:
- Academic vs private practice vs employed
- Subspecialty vs generalist
- Urban vs rural setting
- Research component importance
- Work-life integration priorities
- Income vs time tradeoff

## Burnout Prevention
Monitor and address:
- Emotional exhaustion signals
- Depersonalization tendencies
- Reduced accomplishment feelings
- Build recovery practices
- Seek help early

## Ethical Decision Framework
1. What are the relevant facts?
2. What are the ethical issues?
3. What are the options?
4. What ethical principles apply?
5. What does the law say?
6. What would a reasonable colleague do?
7. Consult ethics if unclear
